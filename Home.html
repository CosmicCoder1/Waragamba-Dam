The Warragamba Dam

Intoduction to Warragamba Dam

The Warragamba Dam is the major dam for Sydney, the dam gets its water from the Wollondilly and Coxs river systems. It took 12 years to build. The dam is located about 65 km west of Sydney, in a narrow gorge of the Warragamba river.

It is a concrete gravity dam, which creates Lake Burragorang - the primary water reservoir for Sydney. It is 142 m high and 351 m long, making it four times larger than Sydney Harbour. This dam provides 80% of Greater Sydney’s portable water supply and has a capacity of 2 027 Gigalitres.
The water supplied by the Warragamba Dam is used in industries and manufacturing the water from the dam also helps to drive the economy as many of these industries and businesses rely on the supply of portable water. This generates billions of dollars of revenue annually. This helps to make many things that we need in our day-to-day lives. The water is also used for agriculture, this water helps to grow many crops which basically means that the water from the dam is used to grow food. The water from Warragamba Dam is also supplied to the urban areas, which supplies tap water to houses and businesses. Other than the above reasons, The water can also be used for recreational purposes, such as swimming pools and water parks. The water that is sourced to these places is from the Warragamba Dam. This dam also has a hydroelectric power station, which generates 50 MW of electricity!
